# Identifying and Defining the Problem

---

## Problem Being Addressed

In aviation, there is a great amount of planning and preparation that must be done prior to flight. This is a very time consuming process and ultimately makes it very hard to plan for flights ahead of schedule. This along with the difficulty and complex nature of things such as reading the METAR (Weather Reports) which could cause the incorrect information to be processed by the pilot especially by a junior pilot. Along with this, the overwhelming nature of multitasking that is required by the pilot causes a significant margin for error. 

## Overview of Software Being Created

To combat this issue, I have thought of creating a program which recieves and reads the weather information for each journey and transcribes it into a more readable version for the pilot. Furthermore, this software will be able to notify the pilot of whether or not this weather and the conditions of flight are suitable for their skill level based of values in categories entered by the pilot.

## Scope and Boundaries

The program will be able to translate the METAR recieved into a more readable version which is easier to follow for the pilots allowing for a correct and more accurate transmission of information. Depending on the difficulty of incorporation, I will aim to incorporate a feature where the pilot needs to solely enter their airport and the program will fetch the METAR from an external free-to-use website.

The program will also be able to provide a "Go/No-Go" to the pilot based on the conditions and comparing that to the personal limitations entered by each pilot. This information would theoretically be stored for each individual user. 

The program may also be able to retrieve stored data of previous sessions and reccomend the user to change their limits or try a more difficult weather routine based on previous experience. This may be achieved through the use of calculated flight hours.

## Ideal Audience and Use

This software would be of best use for up and coming pilots or those who struggle to read METAR reports as they provide quick and efficient translation.

This program's key features include the translation of METAR, confirming if it is within the scope of abilities of the user, tracking flight hours, and reccomendations in training.

## Constraints

Availability and usability of software and hardware is a key factor that may affect the project. With the programming languages and skill level availabel within our syllabus, it may be harder to make the program efficient and fast however, the core parts of it should be abel to be achieved. Furthermore, the lack in hardware makes it challenging to add additional, useful features to the program. These features include the use of NFC tags/readers with pre-loaded information with previous flight data stored.

## Assumptions being made

I will be assuming that the user knows their boundaries and their limitations. Furthermore, it will be calculating the flight hours based on the starting point and the destination. This will also be assuming that the information recieved (METAR) is correct and accurate at the current time.
